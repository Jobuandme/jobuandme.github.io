What I Did

During my grade 12 year in highschool I was in charge of the marketing for our drama production. The poster started off as a project for my digital media class and evolved into the official marketing for our play. I ended up creating the poster, several logos, and shirt designs for the play's crew.

Design Decisions

The colours for the poster and logo are inspired by the pink colour palette from Mean Girls. I stuck to just 3 main colours for the poster to avoid overcomplicating what was planned to be a "messy" design. The poster went through a crazy amount of revisions to make it abide by the copyright standards from the company that owned the rights to Mean Girls. Originally, the poster had plenty of empty space for information, but once I was informed about having to fit the copyright info in, it quickly became cluttered. Fitting the copyright info, as well as the shows info into the poster was a challenge, and I even had to change the logo at the top to fit all the content. Having to fit so much content into a small space honestly elevated the messy chalkboard style of the poster.

Made in Photoshop and Illustrator.

What I Learned

Having to fit so much content into the poster was great practice for structuring designs because I was constantly having to rearrange the entire layout. The whole experience was great for designing within copyright standards, and I learned not to get too attached to any one version of a design.
